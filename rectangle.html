Rectangle

Rectangle is a quadrilateral with four right angles.

It can also be defined as: an equiangular quadrilateral, since equiangular means that all of its angles
are equal (360°/4 = 90°); or a parallelogram containing a right angle.

A rectangle with four sides of equal length is a square. The term oblong is occasionally used to refer to a non-square rectangle.

The word rectangle comes from the Latin rectangulus, which is a combination of rectus (as an adjective, right, proper) and angulus (angle).

A crossed rectangle is a crossed (self-intersecting) quadrilateral which consists of two opposite sides of a rectangle along with the two diagonals (therefore only two sides are parallel).
